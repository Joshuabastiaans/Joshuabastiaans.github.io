WHO I AM

Hi, I'm Joshua. I am a creative game designer with a passion for crafting unique and immersive experiences. Just like other media, games take people outside of their daily lives to escape and connect. My goal is to create games that make you connect and feel what it’s like to be someone else, somewhere else. As a second-year Game Design student at HKU, I'm excited to continue pushing the boundaries of game design and create experiences that resonate deeply with players.

My entire life has been a source of inspiration for my creations. The music I listen to, the movies I watch, and the games I play all significantly influence the games I develop today. When it comes to inspiring my team with a concept, I enjoy creating a simple soundtrack that captures the vibe of the idea. Music conveys emotions and nuances that words often can't describe, and it helps align everyone's perception of the concept, ensuring we're all on the same page.
I also play the drums, and I’ve learned that creating good-sounding drums requires understanding of the entire composition. I believe that the same goes for games. To create effective levels, puzzles or mechanics that suit the game I have to understand the other disciplines that go into the development process of the game. By familiarizing myself with various aspects of the process, I can create more cohesive and engaging experiences.

Experience Design

Isn’t the whole point of making games designing an experience? Well in my "experience," it is often one of the most overlooked areas of design. These are the projects where I focussed creating a new and unique experience.

Project Hybrid

It’s no lie that gaming isn’t the most physically healthy activity to do. Project Hybrid is a game that combines the physical interaction in the real world with the digital world. We made this game with the intention to get people motivated to move by the horror elements of the game. That's why we combined a dance mat with an arcade style horror game. The goal is to escape the haunted arcade hall by moving on the dance mat to the beat of the music.

Role

In this project I had the role of an audio/game designer. This allowed me to focus on matching the mechanics together with the sound feedback. For a game based on rithmic input this was a vital part of making sure the player got the best experience.

Tools

Unity, C#, FMod, A “fake” dance mat

Project Vrij II

What if you could escape the normal consciousness via games? Well, that's how we started this project. Sure, we could drug our players before playing a game but it wouldn’t really be legal (and also maybe not so ethically responsible). That's why we went on a journey to create our own psychedelic experience without the need of any drug.

Role

Together with Lucas, a game designer, we designed and made the whole project. I focused on creating puzzles that represented challenges or thoughts that could occur in a psychedelic trip. I was also responsible for the sound design in this game, making sure you really get immersed in the experience.

Tools

Unity, C#, FMod, Shaders
